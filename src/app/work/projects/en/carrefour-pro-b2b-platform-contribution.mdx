## Context

[Carrefour Pro](https://www.pro.carrefour.fr/) is the B2B arm of the Carrefour group, distributing gift cards to two main segments: company committees and individual retailers, through a network of 600+ stores.

Planet Bourgogne has developed and maintained the dedicated web platform for this business for several years. I contributed as a developer during my first apprenticeship year, working on a live product with high business stakes.

Carrefour Pro generates several hundred million euros in annual revenue and several million orders per year, so the security and reliability stakes of the platform are very high.

## Product

The platform covers the full B2B gift card sales workflow:

- Online ordering portal for company committees and resellers
- Multi-store management for authorized resellers
- Card payment processing across 200+ stores
- Autonomous stock management tools for distributors
- Admin interface for content management

Security is a core constraint: the platform complies with Carrefour group's information security directives and undergoes regular audits.

## My contributions

As a contributing developer on an existing project:

- Delivered new business features on both front-end and back-end, aligned with product priorities.
- Resolved production issues with careful impact analysis before applying any fix.
- Participated in code reviews and pre-release validation cycles.
- Onboarded onto a mature codebase under strict security and quality requirements.

## Stack and practices

- **PHP 8** (Planet Bourgogne internal framework)
- **MySQL**
- **GitLab** — merge requests, code reviews, CI/CD
- **Typesense** — indexing engine
- Agile workflow: sprints, prioritization, continuous feedback

## Challenges and learnings

Taking ownership of an existing, live project with security requirements imposed by a major enterprise client was a formative experience. I learned to measure the impact of every change, communicate clearly on technical decisions, and deliver reliably under real operational pressure.

## Outcome

This experience grounded me in the realities of work on a live product: quality is not just about writing good code — it also depends on process discipline, team collaboration, and the ability to ship consistently without degrading what already works.
